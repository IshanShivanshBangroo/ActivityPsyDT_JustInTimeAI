How this works (quick)

Work in pairs or small groups. You will evaluate two short “AI assisted” messages in health settings: (A) a just in time nudge for cardiac rehab, and (B) a counseling reply that tries to mirror a therapist’s style.

This is a classroom simulation. The example messages are mock samples inspired by the papers, not real medical advice.

Paper cheat sheet (tiny)

You do not need to memorize details. Use this as a reminder for discussion.

Paper A: LLM driven JITAI messaging
  • Idea: LLM can decide when to intervene and write context aware motivational messages for health behavior.
  • Question: if the messages are rated better than humans, does that mean we should automate JITAIs?
  • Big tension: personalization vs safety and accountability.
Paper B: Counselor style digital twins
  • Idea: capture a counselor’s style and generate therapy like replies that feel consistent with that counselor.
  • Question: is “style matching” actually the right goal, or do we need stricter clinical guardrails?
  • Big tension: empathy and rapport vs boundaries and harm prevention.
What to watch for during debate
  • Evidence: what did the evaluations really measure, and what did they skip?
  • Generalization: do results travel to new contexts, new populations, or higher stakes?
  • Failure modes: wrong but persuasive advice, privacy, dependency, equity.
  • Design response: what guardrails, workflows, or audits would you add?
Small group tip: pick one person as “driver” on the laptop and one person as “devil’s advocate”.
